Allergic Emergency Guide

Recognizing Severe Allergic Reaction (Anaphylaxis)

Watch for these symptoms which may appear within seconds or minutes:

  • Difficulty breathing or wheezing
  • Swelling of lips, tongue, or throat
  • Hives, itching, or skin redness
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Dizziness or fainting
  • Nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea
  • Feeling of impending doom

Emergency First Aid

If someone shows signs of a severe allergic reaction:

  • Call emergency services immediately
  • Help the person use an epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) if available
  • Have the person lie down with legs elevated
  • Loosen tight clothing and cover with a blanket
  • If breathing is difficult, allow them to sit up but not stand
  • Do not give anything to drink if having trouble breathing

How to use an EpiPen:

1. Remove safety cap
2. Hold against outer thigh
3. Push firmly until you hear a click
4. Hold in place for 3 seconds
5. Massage injection area for 10 seconds

What To Do After

Even if symptoms improve after epinephrine:

  • Seek immediate medical attention - symptoms may return
  • Stay with the person until help arrives
  • If possible, identify and remove the allergen source
  • Be prepared to administer a second dose if symptoms persist or return
  • Follow up with an allergist for prevention strategies
