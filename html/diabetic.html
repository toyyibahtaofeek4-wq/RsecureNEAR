Diabetic Emergency Assistance

Recognizing Diabetic Emergency

Symptoms of hypoglycemia (low blood sugar):
• Shakiness, dizziness, sweating
• Hunger, irritability
• Confusion, difficulty speaking
• Weakness, sleepiness

Symptoms of hyperglycemia (high blood sugar):
• Frequent urination
• Extreme thirst
• Nausea, vomiting
• Shortness of breath

Emergency Response

If person is conscious:
• For low blood sugar: Give 15g fast-acting carbs (juice, glucose tablets)
• For high blood sugar: Administer insulin if prescribed

If person is unconscious:
• Do not give anything by mouth
• Place in recovery position
• Call emergency services immediately
