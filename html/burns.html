Burns Emergency Guide

Recognizing Burn Severity

First-degree: Red, painful, dry (e.g., sunburn)
Second-degree: Blisters, swelling, moist appearance
Third-degree: White or charred, may be painless (nerve damage)
Seek immediate care for second and third-degree burns

First Aid Treatment

• Cool the burn with cool (not cold) running water for 10-15 minutes
• Cover with a sterile, non-adhesive bandage or clean cloth
• Do not apply ice, butter, or ointments
• Take over-the-counter pain relievers if needed
• Seek medical attention for serious burns
